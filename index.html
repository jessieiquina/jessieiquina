Spatial Analysis of Homicides and the Airbnb Market in Chicago

Homicide Density By Police District

Airbnb Review Density By Police District

Analysis & Methodology

This spatial analysis investigates whether a geographic relationship exists between public safety and the concentration of short-term rental activity in Chicago. By mapping homicide density alongside Airbnb review frequency, the analysis reveals a stark contrast: high-intensity Airbnb activity is clustered in the northeast lakefront districts, while higher homicide rates are primarily concentrated in the central and southwest urban corridors. Furthermore, data integration highlights a clear price disparity, where higher-priced rentals align with lower homicide density near the water. Ultimately, this study demonstrates that while rental market demand is driven by lakefront accessibility, public safety concerns remain most prevalent in inland districts. These findings illustrate the necessity of normalizing spatial data to reveal how socioeconomic factors shape distinct urban experiences across the city.

GIS Tools & Skills Used:
  • Performed spatial analysis by using the Field Calculator to normalize density data.
  • Utilized geoprocessing to verify projections for accurate area-based comparisons.
  • Integrated disparate data sources via Joins, Relates, and Export features.
  • Utilized metadata to verify the context and reliability of the datasets used.

Hispanic Population Density Map in the Contiguous U.S.

1 Dot = 10,000 people

Analysis & Methodology

This map visualizes the spatial distribution of the Hispanic population across the contiguous United States by state[cite: 1]. Utilizing a dot density symbology method, each dot represents 10,000 individuals[cite: 3], allowing for an intuitive understanding of population clusters and regional demographic concentrations. This technique is highly effective for illustrating volume and spatial patterns without the distorting effects sometimes caused by traditional choropleth mapping across variable-sized administrative boundaries.

GIS Tools & Skills Used:
  • Dot Density Symbology: Effectively communicating raw counts and spatial distribution over large geographic extents[cite: 3].
  • Cartographic Design: Balancing dot size and value to ensure regional clusters remain legible at a national scale[cite: 1, 3].
  • Data Symbolization: Translating tabular demographic census data into meaningful visual clusters.

High-Risk Areas for Water Toxicity in Flint, Michigan

Analysis & Methodology

This environmental justice analysis identifies high-risk areas for water toxicity within the ward boundaries of Flint, Michigan[cite: 9, 13]. By overlaying critical infrastructure data, this map highlights zones facing compounding environmental hazards. The spatial layout details the intersection of lead service lines, high-risk exposure areas, and high-pollution facilities generating over 10,000 tons of pollutants near the Michigan river lines[cite: 10, 11, 12].

GIS Tools & Skills Used:
  • Spatial Overlay: Combining multiple infrastructure and hazard layers (lead lines, pollution facilities) to identify vulnerable zones[cite: 10, 12].
  • Multi-Criteria Risk Assessment: Mapping high-risk exposure areas based on overlapping environmental threats[cite: 11].
  • Thematic Mapping: Communicating complex public health and environmental crises using clear, distinct symbology for facilities and water lines[cite: 12].
