Spatial Analysis of Homicides and the Airbnb Market in Chicago

Homicide Density By Police District

Airbnb Review Density By Police District

Analysis & Methodology

This spatial analysis investigates whether a geographic relationship exists between public safety and the concentration of short-term rental activity in Chicago. By mapping homicide density alongside Airbnb review frequency, the analysis reveals a stark contrast: high-intensity Airbnb activity is clustered in the northeast lakefront districts, while higher homicide rates are primarily concentrated in the central and southwest urban corridors. Furthermore, data integration highlights a clear price disparity, where higher-priced rentals align with lower homicide density near the water. Ultimately, this study demonstrates that while rental market demand is driven by lakefront accessibility, public safety concerns remain most prevalent in inland districts. These findings illustrate the necessity of normalizing spatial data to reveal how socioeconomic factors shape distinct urban experiences across the city.

GIS Tools & Skills Used:
  • Performed spatial analysis by using the Field Calculator to normalize density data.
  • Utilized geoprocessing to verify projections for accurate area-based comparisons.
  • Integrated disparate data sources via Joins, Relates, and Export features.
  • Utilized metadata to verify the context and reliability of the datasets used.

Hispanic Population in the Contiguous U.S.

Featured: Hispanic Population Density Map (1 Dot = 10,000 people)

Analysis

The census data is collected by the US Census Bureau every 10 years which is used to determine the seats each state has in the U.S of Representatives which is typically collected through online portals or through other forms of records that the government has. The projection I chose to map the continental US NAD 1983 Contiguous USA Albers because it preserves the area which is important to the data that we are plotting in the US. If used a different projection the states would be projected differently and the data provided would not be plotted correctly especially when using a dot density map where the location and area is needed.

The maps I chose to represent are a Density Dot map, proportion symbol map, and Choropleth map. The Choropleth map shows a gradient of colors where darker colors show a greater percentage of the hispanic population per state. The dot density shows a similar attribute but in terms of quantity and area. The dot represents around 10,000 Hispanic people and looking at the map as a whole you can see where there is a greater population compared to other states. The proportion symbol map uses a singular dot where the size of the dot shows the proportional population size to other states.

There are limitations to using Census Data because it relies on surveying which means there can be an under-count of people especially in those who live in more remote places. Additionally, since Hispanics can be of a multi-ethinic group it can lead to inaccuracies to how people are represented since the census uses fixed categories to define race and ethnicity.

Skills Learned

  • Learned how to retrieve spatial data from the U.S Census Bureau, understanding the way it is labeled, and reinforcing my knowledge on selecting attributes and projections.
  • Developed a deeper understanding of symbology and how to represent different types of maps such as Choropleth and Dot Density Mapping.
  • Learned what values are put in the Normalization option to represent the data correctly, and how to change certain values to help the map read clearly.

High-Risk Areas for Water Toxicity in Flint, Michigan

Analysis & Methodology

This environmental justice analysis identifies high-risk areas for water toxicity within the ward boundaries of Flint, Michigan. By overlaying critical infrastructure data, this map highlights zones facing compounding environmental hazards. The spatial layout details the intersection of lead service lines, high-risk exposure areas, and high-pollution facilities generating over 10,000 tons of pollutants near the Michigan river lines.

GIS Tools & Skills Used:
  • Spatial Overlay: Combining multiple infrastructure and hazard layers (lead lines, pollution facilities) to identify vulnerable zones.
  • Multi-Criteria Risk Assessment: Mapping high-risk exposure areas based on overlapping environmental threats.
  • Thematic Mapping: Communicating complex public health and environmental crises using clear, distinct symbology for facilities and water lines.
